Class of 2025 - Senior Spotlights

Photo

Emily Johnson

GPA: 4.0

Activities: Debate Team Captain, Student Council, Choir

College: Brigham Young University

Emily has been an outstanding student and leader throughout her time at Provo High. As captain of the debate team, she led the group to state championships two years in a row. She plans to study Political Science at BYU and hopes to eventually attend law school. In her free time, Emily enjoys hiking in the nearby mountains and volunteering at the local animal shelter.

Photo

Michael Rodriguez

GPA: 3.95

Activities: Basketball Team, Science Club, Spanish Club

College: University of Utah

Michael has been a standout athlete and scholar at Provo High. He helped lead the basketball team to regional finals while maintaining nearly perfect grades. Michael has received a scholarship to study Computer Science at the University of Utah. He loves coding and has already developed several apps, including one that helps students navigate the school schedule. When not studying or playing basketball, Michael enjoys mountain biking and photography.

Photo

Sarah Thompson

GPA: 3.89

Activities: Orchestra (First Violin), Math Club, Yearbook Editor

College: Utah Valley University

Sarah has been a dedicated musician and scholar during her time at Provo High. As first violin in the school orchestra, she has performed in numerous concerts and competitions. Sarah plans to study Music Education at UVU with the goal of becoming a high school music teacher. She has also been the driving force behind this year's award-winning yearbook. In her spare time, Sarah enjoys reading classic literature and baking desserts for her friends and family.

Photo

David Wilson

GPA: 3.92

Activities: Football Team, Robotics Club President, Eagle Scout

College: Utah State University

David has excelled both on and off the field at Provo High. As a key member of the football team and president of the robotics club, he has demonstrated versatility and leadership. David recently completed his Eagle Scout project, building a new playground for a local elementary school. He will be attending Utah State University on a scholarship to study Mechanical Engineering. When not working on robotics projects, David enjoys fishing, camping, and helping coach youth football.
