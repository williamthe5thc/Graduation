Zoe Deucher

Zoe is our freak of nature child when it comes to strength. She grew up playing soccer and flag football with the boys, and nobody in the family ever wanted to race her. She grew up on a boat so she likes fishing, swimming, water skiing, and wake boarding. During the winter she likes to snow ski.

She is on a club track team that runs year round and on the Timpview track team where she runs sprints and throws javelin. Timpview has won the state championship the last two years in a row and is going for a three peat this year.

In her words, her favorite part of school was that she got to know almost everyone at Timpview and had the chance to make friends from other schools, which made high school fun.

Advice: "Don't worry too much about what others think or the drama that could be going on because it won't matter when you graduate. Be who you are and own it."

Madelyn Peterson

Madelyn has loved to dance since she was a little girl. She started when she was 3 so she's spent the last 15 years of her life at a dance studio. The look on her face when she's dancing sums it all up. It is pure joy!!!

Madelyn plans to attend BYU-I in the fall and then serve a mission next year.

Kate Reichner

Kate is graduating from Timpview and has loved her time there. She has worked so hard in school and in sports. She was on the swim team and the cheer team for all four years. She was also a part of Student Government her junior and senior years. She loved the chance she had to get to know so many people, & has loved her friends and teachers at Timpview.

Kate signed with the Utah Tech swim team, so she is headed to St. George in the fall. She's excited to swim in college- she's worked so hard to achieve that goal.

Kate's advice for younger students is to get involved in clubs or sports to make good friends, and don't forget to have fun along the way! Create strong relationships with your teachers by participating in class and asking for help when needed. Stay organized, set goals, and take care of yourself so you can make the most of your high school experience.

Nick Schooley

When I return, I'll be attending BYU here in Provo.

Some mighty high school Wisdom I can bestow is, if your mom is making you take AP calc, take BC for the first semester, then switch to AB the second semester. You'll learn it much better. Also, avoid going number 2 in the school bathrooms, and don't bum around (too much).

James Wytiaz

Brief high school experience: James challenged himself in high school to take as many advanced courses as possible. He joined the marching band as a freshman and competed all four years, earning multiple awards, distinctions, 1st places, state, regionally, and nationally. James loved to represent the United States during Model United Nations and won Best Resolution this year. He has had the honor of being the trumpet solo for the past 4 Memorial Day and Veterans Day commemorations, and serving as a Trumpeteer at many funerals for veterans. In fact, he is missing this evening to perform his last jazz band concert. James has a love of academics, teaching himself Japanese to go with his fluent Spanish, earning 5s in AP Computer Science, AP Calculus, and more. Along the way he made great friends with his easy-going and bright personality.

Interests and Hobbies: James is a born engineer. Since he arrived earthside he was taking things apart, drawing schematics for his inventions, analyzing why something did or didn't work, making everything more efficient, and building anything from scratch. He is a musician, pianist, and plays a mean trumpet. James loves all things design and architecture. But his true obsession is aviation. He can identify any plane in the sky, asks his parents to send him the itinerary and picture of every plane they fly, so he can tell them if it has the GE or Rolls Royce engine, when the plane rolled off the line, a short history of its maintenance logs, and where the plane has been and where it's going. Any flight we take must account for time to be at the airport for plane-spotting, to meet the pilot and first officers, and ask questions in the cockpit.

Parent Comment: James has been a joy in our family. He is a peacemaker with a steady heart. He serves his family and community with dedication and a happy disposition. He sees needs and fills them. We are so excited to see his future endeavors!

Future Plans: James has committed to the University of Utah with a scholarship, and will defer as he has opened his mission portal.

James advice: Nobody is "smart". To be "smart", you have to be knowledgeable. Take the highest classes you can, even if you procrastinate all of the work. Always look for the highest opportunities. You're more capable than you know, you just have to know how to know first.
