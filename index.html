Zoe Deucher

School: Timpview High School

Activities: Track Team, Flag Football, Water Sports

Future Plans: Study Sports Medicine in College

Zoe is our freak of nature child when it comes to strength. She grew up playing soccer and flag football with the boys, and nobody in the family ever wanted to race her. She grew up on a boat so she likes fishing, swimming, water skiing, and wake boarding. During the winter she likes to snow ski.

She is on a club track team that runs year round and on the Timpview track team where she runs sprints and throws javelin. Timpview has won the state championship the last two years in a row and is going for a three peat this year.

In her words, her favorite part of school was that she got to know almost everyone at Timpview and had the chance to make friends from other schools, which made high school fun.

Advice: "Don't worry too much about what others think or the drama that could be going on because it won't matter when you graduate. Be who you are and own it."

James Wytiaz

Activities: Marching Band, Model United Nations, Jazz Band

Interests: Engineering, Aviation, Music, Architecture

Future Plans: University of Utah with Scholarship, Mission

James challenged himself in high school to take as many advanced courses as possible. He joined the marching band as a freshman and competed all four years, earning multiple awards, distinctions, 1st places, state, regionally, and nationally. James loved to represent the United States during Model United Nations and won Best Resolution this year.

James is a born engineer. Since he arrived earthside he was taking things apart, drawing schematics for his inventions, analyzing why something did or didn't work, making everything more efficient, and building anything from scratch. He is a musician, pianist, and plays a mean trumpet. James loves all things design and architecture. But his true obsession is aviation.

James has committed to the University of Utah with a scholarship, and will defer as he has opened his mission portal.

Advice: "Nobody is 'smart'. To be 'smart', you have to be knowledgeable. Take the highest classes you can, even if you procrastinate all of the work. Always look for the highest opportunities. You're more capable than you know, you just have to know how to know first."
